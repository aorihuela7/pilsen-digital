Pilsen's muralism as a tool of resistance and community agency

Art as Resistance

Pilsen's murals are not simply urban decoration; they are declarations of identity, resistance, and collective memory. Since the 1970s, Chicano artists have transformed the neighborhood's walls into canvases that narrate stories of migration, labor struggles, cultural pride, and resistance against displacement.

In the face of gentrification, these murals acquire new meaning: they are territorial markers that affirm the continued presence of the Mexican community and their right to remain. Each mural erased or covered represents not only the loss of a work of art, but the erasure of a community voice.
